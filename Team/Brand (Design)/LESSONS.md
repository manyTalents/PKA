## Lessons

### General: Match the Owner's Writing Voice
- **Category:** brand, content
- **Lesson:** When ghost-writing as Chris, use the detailed voice profile on file -- his writing has a specific cadence, vocabulary, and rhetorical style that must be matched exactly.
- **Context:** A voice profile was built from Chris's 2021 journal entries. His style is direct, philosophical, densely argued, and layered with trade/business metaphors. Generic "professional" copy does not sound like him. Any content that goes out under his name must pass a voice-match check against the profile.
- **Keywords:** writing voice, ghost-writing, voice profile, Chris, tone, cadence

### 2026-05-19: Two-Audience Rule -- Content Serves Humans AND AI Agents
- **Category:** brand, content strategy
- **Lesson:** Every piece of content must be designed for two audiences: human readers AND AI agents that will parse, index, or operate on the content.
- **Context:** The PKA team operates through AI agents that read, search, and act on content files. Poorly structured content (no headings, no keywords, no consistent formatting) is invisible to the injector, search tools, and cold-start protocols. Content that humans can read but agents cannot parse is only half-functional.
- **Keywords:** two-audience, human, AI agent, content structure, parsing, searchability

### General: Brand Colors Are Established -- Never Deviate
- **Category:** brand, visual identity
- **Lesson:** MTM brand colors are defined and documented; every visual asset, UI component, and marketing material must use the established palette -- no ad hoc color choices.
- **Context:** Brand colors for ManyTalentsMore were established early and are referenced across web, mobile, dashboards, and marketing. Inconsistent colors undermine brand recognition and make the platform look unprofessional. Always pull from the documented palette, never eyeball a shade.
- **Keywords:** brand colors, MTM, palette, visual identity, consistency
